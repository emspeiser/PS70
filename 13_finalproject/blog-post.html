week 5 update

Since my original project idea would be most naturally executed without the use of electronics, I have pivoted to another idea which fulfills requirements more realistically. My new idea is to create a device which gives live-time feedback to skiing technique. I will implement various sensors to measure knee angulation, speed, the slope of the hill, and potentially also pressure in different areas of the foot. With certain preset parameters, such as skill level and the type of skiing event, I can provide live-time feedback via vibrations to indicate areas where a turn can be improved. I’ll make some calculations to understand the relationship between the speed of a turn with ski edge angle in different cases (ie, steep versus flat slope and large versus small radius turns). My inspiration for this project is to determine if an ‘A-frame” (where the inside knee is angulated less than the outside knee in any given turn) is faster or slower than both knees being angulated the same amount. It’s still debated today which one is faster than the other, so this project can double as a device which corrects form, as well as research into racing technique.

The grey box will house components, such as a small breadboard, accelerometer, and microcontroller. The vibrator motors will also be housed in the 3D printed box, but if I decide to scale up and use two (to denote too much or too little edge angle), then I will need to make another compartment and use a bluetooth module.
